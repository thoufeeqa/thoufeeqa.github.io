Facial Feature Recognition using OpenCV

This project was part of an overall video analytics research project between the Bhaba Atomic Research Centre, Mumbaiand the AU-KBC research centre at the Madras Institute of Technology. My research group was in charge of the face recognition system.

Normal face recognition systems are vulnerable to changes in lighting and pose. We decided to combine the strengths of two models, the Dense Scale Invariant Transform (DSIFT) and the Deformable Parts Model (DPM). Using the strengths of both these models improved recognition robustness in low light conditions, and upto a 30 degree tilt in head pose.

The project was a collaborative effort between two other undergraduate researchers, a postgraduate guide, a professor who was our research guide, and a research representative from BARC, Mumbai.

Since this was an undergraduate research project, the thesis proposal and development was carried out by my peers and I, with the rest of the team playing a guiding role.

The source code is of two parts, one trains the system and the other is the one deployed to recognize faces.

Venture deal information extractor

This is an early prototype of the information extraction system I developed during my time at Termsheet.

The overall system consists of three parts : various crawlers that extracted articles from websites, news sources and RSS feeds on the internet, a classfifer that decided if an articles was related to venture deals, and a tagger that extracted relevant information such as the startup being funded, amount of money invested, the founders and the firm funding them, among other things.

Information extracted from the tagger allowed us to quickly filter and keep track of deals taking place globally

The classifier implements a Random Forest classifier that operates on words weighted by the Tf-idf function (term frequency, inverse document frequency). Basically, words appearing multiple times in an article are weighted as more important (Term frequency). Inverse document frequency offsets these weights based on the frequency of words that appear more frequently in general (words like 'the', 'and'). The Random forest classifier has to be trained initially and uses these term weights to decides if an article belongs in a set or not.

The tagger implements Stanford's NER library (Named Entity Recognition). This model also has to be trained first. Long hours were spent intially training the tagger to extract items that were important to us in funding articles. A system was then created to allow us to edit articles tagged incorrectly, and feed the corrections back into the training source to improve accuracy over time.

Rover Rover - A Memory Game (Game Jam Project)

This was one of my early pet projects, built in Unity

I took part in a game jam with a time limit of four hours and a theme centered around memory games.

The aim is to manoeuvre a rover across a terrain with obstacles placed randomly. The catch however, is that you need to pre-program a set of instructions to the rover before it starts moving; a nod to how engineers have to send a set of instructions to execute, due to the amount of time it takes for signals to travel from the earth to the Moon...or Mars.

The player is given 3 lives per level and the obstacles on each level are procedurally generated. The player explores the terrain and has to memorise the correct sequence of instructions to give (Right, Left or Forward) as they progress and get to the end of the stage.

The rover and the obstacles were modelled by me, using Blender. I don't know how to animate that well, so each of the axles in the rover are rotated using code instead and the rover body is parented to these axles. So when it's turning, there's basically one section of the code manipulating the rover's overall movement, and another section of code that's rotating the axles at the same time.

Tesselated Landscape Materials

Normal maps don't work very well in VR, especially at close range or when viewed head on. This is a tesselated set of materials that can be painted onto a landscape. Tesselation is applied only at close range, and after that it eases out gradually until normal maps take over at longer distances from the camera. Height maps were used to make the blend between the types of terrain look smoother. This maintained performance while adding more depth to the terrain.

Waterfall Material

Using particle effects to make a waterfall was pretty but computationally demanding

Instead, I made a waterfall shader that closely mimics it. This applied on a curved mesh.

The material has diffuse and normal maps for large, medium and small waves. This is so that that the water looks like it has some depth to it. Smaller waves travel faster on the surface, and tha larger ones travel slower to make it look like they're deeper.

The material starts with a blue color tint but turns white towards the edge of the waterfall to mimic the way water goes from calm to chaotic at the edge. This is completely editable within the material too. The clipping point can be shifted up or down depending on the curvature of the mesh. Wave speed and sizes can be changed, both in the horizontal and vertical directions.

The only place it falls short is around rocks placed in the water. I've used scene depth effects to mimic some chaos aorund the rock, but this still needs more work to improve realism.
